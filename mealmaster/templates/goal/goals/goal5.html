Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

What is BMR and how important it is to health?

Calculating BMR helps you know the minimum amount of calories or energy your body needs to perform basic bodily functions such as breathing, digesting food, pumping blood, regulating body temperature, growing hair, producing or repairing body cells, and maintaining the right levels of essential chemicals in your body.

In addition, studies in mice have found that mice with low BMR tend to gain weight and may be more prone to insulin resistance, while those with high BMR may help delay the onset of type 2 diabetes. However, the results still need to be studied further.
